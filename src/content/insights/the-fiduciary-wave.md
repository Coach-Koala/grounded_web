title: "The fiduciary wave: your health plan is now litigation surface"
date: "2026-07-14"
summary: "ERISA class actions over health plan spending have arrived. The employers getting sued aren't the ones who looked at their data — they're the ones who didn't."
---

For twenty years, ERISA fiduciary litigation meant retirement plans:
excessive 401(k) fees, imprudent fund menus, familiar territory.

That playbook has now arrived at the health plan. High-profile class
actions have been filed against major employers alleging fiduciary breaches
in health benefit spending — prescription drug pricing that ignored cheaper
equivalents, broker and TPA compensation that was never scrutinized, plan
costs that no prudent process ever reviewed. The same firms that built the
retirement-fee litigation wave are recruiting health plan plaintiffs today.

The legal theory is simple: **as a self-funded plan sponsor, you are a
fiduciary.** The Consolidated Appropriations Act of 2021 sharpened the
duties — it requires brokers and consultants to disclose their
compensation, bans gag clauses that hide your own claims data from you, and
expects prudent oversight of plan spending.

Which creates an uncomfortable syllogism:

1. You now have the legal right to your claims, pricing, and compensation
   data.
2. Courts increasingly expect a prudent fiduciary to use it.
3. "We never looked" is becoming a liability statement, not an excuse.

The good news: the defense and the savings are the same activity. A
documented, independent review of claims, contracts, and vendor
compensation is simultaneously a prudent-process record and the fastest way
to find money that's leaking. Employers who look first get both; employers
who wait get discovery.

You don't need a litigation posture to start. You need a process — and a
record that it ran.
