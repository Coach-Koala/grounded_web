Most self-insured employers have never had an independent party look at their
own plan data. Not the TPA's summary. Not the broker's renewal deck. The
actual claims.

When someone finally looks, the same categories show up again and again:

- **Billing errors that were paid anyway.** Duplicate claims, unbundled
  charges, and coding errors don't get caught by default — catching them has
  to be someone's job.
- **Contract terms working against you.** Language agreed to years ago that
  nobody has re-read since, priced out of line with what your peers pay.
- **Vendors coasting.** Point solutions that promised engagement and savings,
  reported neither, and got renewed anyway.

None of this requires a big data lift from your team. A scorecard is the
high-level read: how your plan compares, where the critical findings are, and
whether a deeper claims review would pay for itself.
